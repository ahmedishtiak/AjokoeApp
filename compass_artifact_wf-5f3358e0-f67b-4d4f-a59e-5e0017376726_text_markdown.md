# Finnish Driving Theory Test "Traffic Situations" — Complete Scenario Inventory for 100+ Yes/No Practice Questions

## TL;DR
- The category B Traficom theory test contains exactly **50 image-based "traffic situations" (kuvakysymykset) answered Yes/No, with ~10 seconds per question and a maximum of 7 wrong allowed** (43/50 to pass) — so your app's section should mirror that format; this report supplies 140+ distinct, rule-correct scenarios across all 12 requested areas.
- Every scenario is grounded in the **Tieliikennelaki 729/2018** (in force 1 June 2020) and authoritative interpretation (Traficom, Liikenneturva, Väylä, Poliisi, Autoliitto), with the correct Yes/No answer and the exact rule/numeric threshold stated for each.
- The highest-yield "trap" rules to build around: give-way to the right at unmarked junctions; turning driver always yields to pedestrians/cyclists/mopeds crossing the road being entered; roundabout exit = signal right + yield to crossing light traffic; yard/parking exit yields to all; bus leaving a stop has priority where the limit is ≤60 km/h; tram priority even from the left; 5 m no-stopping from crossings; winter tyres 3.0 mm (1.11.–31.3.); drink-drive limit 0.5‰.

## Key Findings

**Test format (authoritative, Traficom).** The category B theory test is taken on a tablet at an Ajovarma service point, lasts **30 minutes**, and has three separately-scored sections. Traficom states verbatim: *"The theory test takes 30 minutes. There are three types of questions: multiple choice questions, questions related to images showing traffic situations, and risk identification questions. You will need to score a specific number of points for each section."* The B-class split is **15 multiple-choice (pass ≥11), 50 image-based traffic-situation questions (pass ≥43, i.e. max 7 wrong), and 5 risk-identification questions (pass ≥4, max 1 wrong)** — 70 questions total, and all three sections must be passed independently (you cannot offset a weak section with a strong one). Historical official guidance (AKE/Trafi Teoriakoeohje 2002 and the Trafi opetussuunnitelma) sets the answer window at **10 seconds per image question** (30 s for verbal), plus a 3-second grace period; the current Traficom page publishes only the 30-minute overall limit, so treat the 10-second figure as well-established convention rather than a currently-published number.

**The distinctive Finnish sign detail.** Unlike most Vienna Convention countries that use white backgrounds, Finnish **warning signs (triangular) and prohibitory/speed-limit signs (round) use a YELLOW background** with red borders — except the no-parking/no-standing signs, which are blue. This is correct to highlight in app explanations as a uniquely Finnish identifier.

**Core legal anchors.** Give-way duties are set in TLL §24 (yield to the right; always yield when entering from yard/parking/path/minor road; always yield when entering the carriageway after crossing a footway/cycleway). The turning driver's duty toward pedestrians, cyclists and mopeds crossing the road being entered is absolute and independent of road markings. The pedestrian-crossing duty is §27 (approach at a speed allowing a stop; give free passage to a pedestrian on or about to step onto the zebra). Bus-leaving-stop priority (§26) applies where the limit is ≤60 km/h — confirmed verbatim by Traficom: *"On roads whose speed limit is at most 60 km/h, buses leaving a bus stop must be given way even if there is a bicycle lane next to the stop."* Tram priority at intersections is in §14/§24 (yield even from the left). Emergency-vehicle free passage applies to vehicles giving **both** audible and visual alarm signals.

Below is the full inventory, organised by your 12 areas. Each line gives a driver's-eye scenario, the correct Yes/No answer to the proposal, and the governing rule.

## Details

### 1. PRIORITY / RIGHT-OF-WAY (TLL §24, §11, §14)

1. **Unmarked equal crossroads, a car approaches from your right.** Proposal: "I must give way." → **YES.** Basic rule: yield to the vehicle approaching from the right when no signs/markings (§24.1).
2. **Unmarked equal crossroads, a car approaches from your left.** Proposal: "I must give way to it." → **NO.** You have priority over the left; but you must still yield to a tram from either direction.
3. **You face a give-way triangle (kärkikolmio, yellow with red border).** Proposal: "I must give way to all traffic on the crossing road." → **YES.** B5 give-way sign.
4. **You face a STOP sign (B6).** Proposal: "I may roll through slowly if the road looks clear." → **NO.** Mandatory full stop at the stop line / before the sign, then yield.
5. **You are on a priority road (etuajo-oikeutettu tie, B1 — yellow diamond).** Proposal: "Vehicles from the side road must give way to me." → **YES.**
6. **Priority-road sign passed; no end sign yet.** Proposal: "I still have priority at the next junction." → **YES.** Priority continues until an end-of-priority sign.
7. **Turning left across oncoming traffic at a junction.** Proposal: "I must give way to oncoming vehicles going straight." → **YES** (§24).
8. **Turning right at a junction, a pedestrian is crossing the side road you turn into.** Proposal: "I must give way to the pedestrian." → **YES.** Turning driver yields to pedestrians/cyclists/mopeds crossing the road being entered.
9. **Turning left, a cyclist crosses the road you are turning into on a cycle crossing.** Proposal: "I must give way to the cyclist." → **YES.** Turning driver always yields regardless of markings.
10. **T-junction: you are on the terminating (stem) road, no signs.** Proposal: "The through road has priority automatically because mine ends." → **NO.** Without signs the right-hand rule still governs; road shape/size does not create priority.
11. **Narrow road, an obstacle (parked car) on your side.** Proposal: "I must give way to oncoming traffic." → **YES** (§24: the side with the obstacle yields).
12. **A tram approaches the equal junction from your left.** Proposal: "I may proceed because it is on my left." → **NO.** Trams get free passage at junctions from any direction (§14/§24).
13. **You exit a yard/courtyard (piha) onto the street.** Proposal: "I must give way to all road users, including pedestrians." → **YES** (§24.3: yield to all when entering from yard/parking/petrol station/path/minor road).
14. **You leave a parking area (pysäköintialue) onto the road.** Proposal: "Cyclists on the crossing cycle path must give way to me." → **NO.** You must give way to all other traffic.
15. **You drive onto the carriageway after crossing a footway/cycleway (structural exit).** Proposal: "I must give way to all road users." → **YES** (§24.3 item 4 — the distinctively new 2020 rule).
16. **Living-street/yard-street (pihakatu) exit onto a normal street.** Proposal: "I must yield to traffic on the street." → **YES.**
17. **Equal crossroads, two cars arrive simultaneously, one from your right and you intend straight.** Proposal: "I proceed first." → **NO.** Yield to the right.
18. **A "side road on the right" warning sign (A21-type, yellow).** Proposal: "This sign alone gives me priority." → **NO.** It only warns; without a priority sign the right-hand rule applies (in built-up areas an equal junction is warned by the tienristeys sign when the limit is ≥50).

### 2. ROUNDABOUTS (Liikenneturva / TLL §24)

19. **Approaching a roundabout with a give-way triangle.** Proposal: "I must give way to vehicles already circulating." → **YES.**
20. **Entering the roundabout.** Proposal: "I should signal left or right on entry." → **NO.** No signal on entry (unless leaving immediately at the first exit — then signal right early).
21. **Exiting the roundabout.** Proposal: "I must signal right before my exit." → **YES.** Exiting = turning right; signal right in good time.
22. **Exiting the roundabout, a pedestrian is on the zebra across your exit.** Proposal: "I must give way to the pedestrian." → **YES.** Exiting driver yields to crossing pedestrians/cyclists/mopeds.
23. **Entering the roundabout, a cyclist crosses your entry on the cycle crossing.** Proposal: "I must give way to the crossing cyclist." → **YES.**
24. **Single-lane roundabout, you want a far exit.** Proposal: "I stay in the single circulating lane and signal right only before exiting." → **YES.**
25. **Two-lane roundabout, near (first) exit.** Proposal: "I choose the right-hand lane on approach." → **YES.** Far exits use the left/inner lane, then change out lane-by-lane with signalling.
26. **Long vehicle ahead swinging wide in the roundabout.** Proposal: "I may overtake it on the inside as it circulates." → **NO.** Give large vehicles room; do not draw alongside.

### 3. TRAFFIC LIGHTS (Liikennevaloasetus / TLL)

27. **Steady red.** Proposal: "I must stop before the stop line." → **YES.**
28. **Steady yellow and you are already so close you cannot stop safely.** Proposal: "I may continue past the line." → **YES** (only if a safe stop is impossible).
29. **Steady yellow, you can stop comfortably.** Proposal: "I may accelerate through." → **NO.** Yellow = stop unless unable to do so safely.
30. **Red + yellow together.** Proposal: "The light is about to turn green; I may start moving now." → **NO.** Prepare to go, but do not move until green.
31. **Green, turning left.** Proposal: "I must give way to oncoming traffic and to pedestrians/cyclists crossing." → **YES.** A round green gives no priority over them.
32. **Green, turning right, pedestrians cross the road you turn into.** Proposal: "I must give way to them." → **YES.**
33. **Green ARROW (nuolivalo) in your direction.** Proposal: "I can proceed knowing crossing pedestrians and oncoming traffic have red." → **YES.** Arrow means no conflicting traffic — but still watch for emergency vehicles.
34. **Flashing yellow (keltavilkku) at a junction.** Proposal: "Normal give-way rules and any give-way signs apply." → **YES.** Lights are out of use; follow signs/right-hand rule.
35. **Flashing red.** Proposal: "I may pass the signal/stop line without stopping." → **NO.** Flashing red = must not pass the signal or stop line.
36. **You have a green light AND a give-way sign at the same junction.** Proposal: "While green, I must still obey the give-way sign." → **YES** (per Autoliitto practice questions — the sign governs when relevant).
37. **White X (red cross) over your lane on a gantry.** Proposal: "I may keep using this lane." → **NO.** Lane closed; move to a lane showing a green down-arrow.
38. **Public-transport/tram signal (white bar signals) shows "stop" bar.** Proposal: "It applies to me as a car driver." → **NO.** These joukkoliikenne/tram signals apply to trams/buses; a car follows its own vehicle signals. (Build as a recognition item.)
39. **Pedestrian light shows flashing green.** Proposal: "As a driver I may already enter the crossing." → **NO.** Pedestrian phase still protected; a red-protection (punasuoja) interval follows.

### 4. PEDESTRIANS & CYCLISTS (TLL §27, §24, §18)

40. **A pedestrian is standing at the kerb about to step onto a zebra.** Proposal: "I must give way." → **YES** (§27: free passage to a pedestrian on or about to step onto the crossing).
41. **Approaching a zebra at speed with a pedestrian waiting.** Proposal: "I must approach at a speed allowing me to stop before it." → **YES** (§27).
42. **A car has stopped at the zebra in the next lane.** Proposal: "I may pass it without stopping." → **NO.** Do not pass a vehicle stopped for a crossing unless a free lane/refuge separates you.
43. **Pedestrian on a refuge island mid-crossing, moving toward your side.** Proposal: "I must give way as they continue." → **YES.**
44. **A child is alone near the roadside/at a crossing.** Proposal: "I must exercise special caution and be ready to stop." → **YES** (special-caution duty near children, elderly, disabled).
45. **A person with a white cane / guide dog at the roadside.** Proposal: "I must give way and exercise special caution." → **YES.** White cane signals a visually impaired pedestrian.
46. **An elderly person crossing slowly.** Proposal: "I may hurry them by edging forward." → **NO.** Special caution; give free passage.
47. **A cyclist rides across on a marked cycle crossing (pyörätien jatke) where YOU face a give-way triangle.** Proposal: "I must give way to the cyclist." → **YES.**
48. **A cyclist rides across the cycle crossing but there is NO give-way sign for you and you are going straight (not turning).** Proposal: "I must give way to the cyclist." → **NO.** Without a sign, the cyclist entering from a cycle path yields; markings alone don't shift priority.
49. **You are TURNING and a cyclist crosses the road you turn into (on a cycle crossing).** Proposal: "I must give way." → **YES.** Turning driver always yields, regardless of signs.
50. **A person is pushing (walking) a bicycle across the zebra.** Proposal: "I must give way as to a pedestrian." → **YES.** A walked bicycle = pedestrian.
51. **A cyclist is riding (not walking) across a plain zebra, no cycle-crossing markings, you go straight with no give-way sign.** Proposal: "I must give way." → **NO** (cyclist yields), but you must not endanger them.
52. **Overtaking a cyclist on a two-way road.** Proposal: "I must leave a safe lateral clearance and may use the oncoming side if it is clear." → **YES** (§18/§24 require giving the cyclist safe space; overtaking may use the opposite side when only one lane each way).
53. **A moped is in the cycle lane (pyöräkaista).** Proposal: "Mopeds may ride in the cycle lane." → **NO.** Cycle lanes are for cyclists; mopeds are not allowed.
54. **Turning across a cycle path that runs alongside your road.** Proposal: "I must give way to cyclists on that path." → **YES** (turning/crossing driver yields to cyclists using the path edge).
55. **A bus stop you are passing; a bus is dropping passengers.** Proposal: "A passenger may step out in front; I must anticipate and give way to alighting passengers." → **YES** (anticipation duty; see also tram §15).

### 5. OVERTAKING & LANE DISCIPLINE (TLL §17–19)

56. **Solid centre line (sulkuviiva) on your side.** Proposal: "I may cross it to overtake." → **NO.**
57. **Dashed centre line.** Proposal: "Overtaking is allowed if it is safe." → **YES.**
58. **Combined line: solid on your side, dashed on the other.** Proposal: "I (with the solid line) may NOT cross to overtake." → **YES** (the line nearest you governs).
59. **At a pedestrian crossing.** Proposal: "I may overtake using the oncoming side at the zebra." → **NO** (forbidden to overtake on the oncoming side at/immediately before a crossing).
60. **On or just before a hill crest / blind bend.** Proposal: "I may overtake if I am quick." → **NO.** Forbidden where sight distance is insufficient.
61. **At/just before an intersection (sign-marked, in a built-up area).** Proposal: "Overtaking is forbidden." → **YES.** (Outside built-up areas the ban applies only to sign-indicated junctions; minor paths/field roads are exempt.)
62. **A vehicle ahead is turning left and has signalled and moved to the centre.** Proposal: "I may pass it on the right." → **YES.** Pass left-turning vehicles on the right.
63. **The vehicle ahead has signalled to overtake the vehicle in front of it.** Proposal: "I may begin overtaking it." → **NO.** Don't overtake a vehicle that is itself starting to overtake.
64. **Multi-lane road in your direction.** Proposal: "I must keep to the rightmost lane unless it causes danger/inconvenience to use another." → **YES** (drive in the rightmost lane; don't change lanes unnecessarily).
65. **Two lanes merge (zip-merge sign — red two-branch arrow on blue).** Proposal: "Drivers take turns (zipper) to merge." → **YES.**
66. **A tram is moving ahead on rails in your lane.** Proposal: "I overtake it on the right." → **YES** (trams are overtaken on the right unless rail position requires otherwise / one-way street).

### 6. SPEED & SIGNS (TLL §25; default limits)

67. **Built-up-area sign (taajama, E22) and no other limit.** Proposal: "The limit is 50 km/h." → **YES.**
68. **Outside built-up areas, no posted limit.** Proposal: "The general limit is 80 km/h." → **YES.**
69. **Motorway with no higher posted figure on entry ramp.** Proposal: "I may immediately drive 120 km/h." → **NO.** 80 km/h applies until a 100 or 120 sign appears; max motorway limit is 120.
70. **Yard street / living street (pihakatu, E24).** Proposal: "I must adapt to walking pace, max 20 km/h." → **YES.**
71. **Pedestrian street (kävelykatu, E26) where driving is exceptionally allowed.** Proposal: "Max speed is 20 km/h and walking pace." → **YES.**
72. **A speed-limit sign with a YELLOW background.** Proposal: "This is a normal Finnish speed-limit sign." → **YES.** Finnish prohibitory/limit signs use yellow backgrounds.
73. **End-of-speed-limit / end-of-zone (C35) in a built-up area.** Proposal: "The town's general 50 km/h resumes." → **YES** (unless on another posted zone).
74. **You turn off onto another road in a built-up area, no new sign.** Proposal: "The general 50 km/h applies." → **YES.** Turning resets to the general limit unless signed; going straight, the limit continues.
75. **Winter, motorway with seasonal limits.** Proposal: "120 may drop to 100 in winter." → **YES** (and many 100 roads drop to 80).
76. **A red-bordered yellow round sign with a number.** Proposal: "It is only a recommended speed." → **NO.** Round red-bordered = mandatory limit; recommendation signs are a different (square) format.
77. **Minimum-speed sign (new 2020 sign).** Proposal: "I must not drive below the shown speed unless traffic requires." → **YES.**

### 7. WINTER / WEATHER / HAZARD (TLL §3, §36, §104–106)

78. **Black ice / slippery conditions.** Proposal: "I must adjust speed below the posted limit for conditions." → **YES.** The limit is a maximum, not a safe situational speed.
79. **Winter tyres, season.** Proposal: "Winter tyres (≥3.0 mm tread) must be used 1 November–31 March when weather/road conditions require." → **YES.** Confirmed verbatim by Traficom: *"In Finland, cars and vans must be fitted with winter tyres during the period from 1 November to 31 March if the weather or road surface conditions so require... The depth of the principal tread grooves of passenger car winter tyres must be at least 3.0 mm."* (Summer tyres min 1.6 mm.)
80. **Studded tyres (nastarenkaat).** Proposal: "They may be used 1 Nov–31 March and otherwise when conditions require." → **YES** (may keep until the Monday after Easter if later than mid-April).
81. **From 1 Dec 2024, a winter tyre marked only "M+S."** Proposal: "M+S alone still qualifies as a winter tyre." → **NO.** Per Traficom Senior Inspector Reetta Kinisjärvi: *"From the beginning of December, the symbol is mandatory for passenger car and van non-studded winter tyres... you cannot use non-studded winter tyres that do not have the three-peak mountain snowflake symbol in winter conditions."* It must carry the 3PMSF "alpine" symbol or be studded (or POR).
82. **Dense fog.** Proposal: "Full-beam (kaukovalo) headlights improve my vision." → **NO.** In fog, high beam reflects back; use dipped beam (and fog lights are permitted only in fog/heavy rain or snow).
83. **Front fog lights.** Proposal: "I may use them only in fog or heavy rain/snow." → **YES.**
84. **Heavy rain after a dry spell / standing water.** Proposal: "Aquaplaning risk is higher and I should slow down." → **YES.** First rain lifts oil/dust; worn tyres worsen aquaplaning.
85. **You feel the car aquaplane.** Proposal: "I should brake hard and steer sharply." → **NO.** Ease off the accelerator, avoid sudden steering/braking, keep direction.
86. **Low winter sun glare.** Proposal: "I should reduce speed and increase following distance." → **YES.**
87. **Slippery bridge in freezing conditions.** Proposal: "Bridges can ice over before the rest of the road." → **YES.**
88. **Following distance in winter.** Proposal: "I should keep a longer gap than the dry-road minimum." → **YES.** Dry-road maantie guidance is about a 3–4 second gap (Liikenneturva); winter requires more.
89. **A skid (rear slides out).** Proposal: "I steer toward where I want to go and avoid abrupt braking." → **YES.**
90. **Daytime running.** Proposal: "I must use dipped headlights or daytime running lights at all times when driving." → **YES** (§36). In tunnels/poor visibility, DRLs are insufficient — use dipped beam.

### 8. WILDLIFE & RURAL (Liikenneturva / Riistakeskus / Fintraffic)

91. **Moose/elk warning sign (yellow triangle).** Proposal: "I should reduce speed and watch the verges, especially at dawn/dusk." → **YES.** Per Fintraffic's Eero Sauramäki: *"There were over 1,650 elk collisions in Finland last year... Most elk accidents occur at dawn and dusk in the autumn"*; insurer Pohjola reports nearly 40% of all yearly animal collisions occur October–December.
92. **An elk runs onto the road ahead.** Proposal: "I should try to pass behind it (aim for its rear)." → **YES.** Elk move forward; brake with pumping action, avoid swerving into oncoming traffic.
93. **Deer (peura/kauris) warning sign.** Proposal: "Deer may bound unpredictably in several directions." → **YES.** Lower your gaze along the verge; deer/roe-deer are less predictable than elk.
94. **You see one reindeer cross in Lapland.** Proposal: "Others may follow; I should slow down." → **YES.** Reindeer move in herds.
95. **You hit or see an injured large animal (elk/deer/reindeer/boar/large predator).** Proposal: "I must report it to 112." → **YES.**
96. **Narrow rural road, soft verge.** Proposal: "I may pull fully onto the verge to pass." → **NO/Caution.** Soft verges can give way; do not assume the shoulder is firm.
97. **Hunters seen near the road.** Proposal: "I should reduce speed (game may be flushed across)." → **YES.**

### 9. MOTORWAY / EXPRESSWAY (TLL §37, §62; motorway rules)

98. **Joining the motorway from an acceleration lane.** Proposal: "I must give way to vehicles already on the motorway." → **YES.** The joining driver yields; through traffic need not move over.
99. **Through traffic with a vehicle joining from a ramp.** Proposal: "I am required to change to the left lane to let it in." → **NO.** No duty to give way; keeping a safe gap is the courteous method.
100. **Normal driving on the motorway.** Proposal: "I keep to the rightmost free lane." → **YES.** Left lanes are for overtaking.
101. **Hard shoulder (piennar).** Proposal: "I may drive along it to bypass a queue." → **NO.** It is for emergencies only.
102. **Stopping/standing on the motorway.** Proposal: "I may stop on the carriageway to check the map." → **NO.** Stopping/standing/reversing prohibited except for a forced obstacle, give-way duty, or emergency.
103. **Reversing on a motorway.** Proposal: "I may reverse to a missed exit." → **NO.**
104. **Exiting the motorway.** Proposal: "I move to the right lane in good time and use the deceleration lane." → **YES.**
105. **Minimum-speed / "too slow" vehicles.** Proposal: "A vehicle whose design speed is too low for the road may not use it." → **YES** (mopeds, tractors, etc. excluded).
106. **Following distance at 120 km/h.** Proposal: "I should keep a clearly longer gap than at town speeds." → **YES.** At 120 km/h you cover ~33 m per second.
107. **A breakdown on the motorway.** Proposal: "I should pull onto the shoulder, use hazard lights, place the warning triangle well back, and wear a hi-vis vest." → **YES.** Towing by rope is forbidden except to the next exit.

### 10. PARKING / STOPPING (TLL §37–38)

108. **No-parking sign (blue with red diagonal).** Proposal: "Brief stopping to load/drop off is generally still allowed." → **YES.** No-parking forbids leaving the car standing; brief stopping is permitted.
109. **No-standing/no-stopping sign (blue with red cross).** Proposal: "I may not even stop here except in emergency/forced stop." → **YES.**
110. **Within 5 m of a crossing road's nearest edge.** Proposal: "Parking/stopping is forbidden." → **YES.** The 5 m rule applies at junctions and before crossings.
111. **Less than 5 m before a zebra / cycle crossing.** Proposal: "I may park there." → **NO.** Forbidden within 5 m before a crossing (from the main approach direction).
112. **On a hill crest or blind bend.** Proposal: "Stopping/parking is forbidden." → **YES.**
113. **In a roundabout.** Proposal: "I may stop briefly." → **NO.** Stopping/parking forbidden in a roundabout.
114. **On a cycle lane or bus lane.** Proposal: "Parking is forbidden." → **YES.**
115. **At a marked bus stop.** Proposal: "I may park to wait for a passenger." → **NO.** Stop only briefly to pick up/drop off where allowed; parking is forbidden at the stop.
116. **Parking-disc (pysäköintikiekko) zone.** Proposal: "I must display my arrival time clearly (disc set to arrival or the next half/full hour)." → **YES.** The new law allows any clear display, not only an official disc.
117. **Two-way street in a built-up area.** Proposal: "I may park on the left side too." → **YES** (allowed in towns since 2020, if it doesn't endanger/obstruct and you don't cross a barrier line).
118. **Outside a built-up area on a road signed as a priority road.** Proposal: "Parking on the carriageway is forbidden." → **YES.**
119. **Within 30 m of a railway level crossing.** Proposal: "Parking is forbidden." → **YES.**
120. **Alongside another stopped car (double-parking).** Proposal: "I may park beside it." → **NO.** Parking beside an already-parked vehicle (other than a two-wheeler) is forbidden.
121. **At a solid line with less than 3 m between car and line and no dashed line between.** Proposal: "Stopping is forbidden." → **YES.**

### 11. EMERGENCY / SPECIAL (TLL §6, §15, §26; level crossings)

122. **An emergency vehicle approaches with blue lights AND siren.** Proposal: "I must give it free passage, even moving on at a red light if it is the only safe way." → **YES.** Free passage is required for vehicles giving both audible and visual signals; you may carefully cross a red to clear the way if it is safe.
123. **A police car behind you shows only a blue light, no siren.** Proposal: "I must run the red light to clear the way." → **NO.** Without the siren you must not break traffic rules to give way; never drive through red on lights alone.
124. **A bus at a stop on a road with limit ≤60 km/h signals to pull out.** Proposal: "I must give way to the departing bus." → **YES** (§26).
125. **A bus at a stop on a road with limit 80 km/h signals to pull out.** Proposal: "I must give way." → **NO.** The duty applies only where the limit is ≤60 km/h.
126. **A tram has stopped at a stop with no passenger island; passengers are alighting onto the roadway.** Proposal: "I must stop and let them cross." → **YES** (§15: give free passage to boarding/alighting tram passengers where there is no island).
127. **A tram stopped at a stop, signalling right, no island.** Proposal: "I may overtake it on the right." → **NO.** Passengers may step out; wait.
128. **Guarded level crossing, barriers lowering / red light.** Proposal: "I must stop and wait." → **YES.**
129. **Unguarded level crossing.** Proposal: "I must exercise special caution and check for a train regardless of any protection." → **YES.**
130. **Roadworks with a traffic controller (liikenteenohjaaja).** Proposal: "I must obey the controller's signals even over traffic lights/signs." → **YES.** A traffic controller overrides lights and signs.
131. **Temporary roadworks signs conflict with permanent signs.** Proposal: "Temporary signs take precedence." → **YES.**
132. **A funeral procession / police-led escort giving signals.** Proposal: "I should give way and not break it up." → **YES** (a column led by a police vehicle giving alarm signals gets free passage).

### 12. RISK PERCEPTION (commonly tested judgement items)

133. **A "stale" green light you've watched stay green a long time as you approach.** Proposal: "I should be ready for it to turn yellow and cover the brake." → **YES.** Watch pedestrian signals as a cue.
134. **A ball rolls into the road from between parked cars.** Proposal: "I should brake and expect a child to follow." → **YES.**
135. **Parked cars line your side and a pedestrian could be hidden between them.** Proposal: "I should reduce speed and increase my margin." → **YES.**
136. **You pass a row of parked cars with people inside.** Proposal: "A door could open; I should keep clearance and slow down." → **YES.** ("Dutch reach" anticipation.)
137. **A double-parked van ahead with no visible driver.** Proposal: "Someone may step out from in front of it; I should slow and be ready." → **YES.**
138. **An oncoming vehicle drifts over the centre line toward you.** Proposal: "I should slow, move right, and be ready to use the verge." → **YES.**
139. **Brake lights flare repeatedly far ahead (a queue forming).** Proposal: "I should lift off and increase following distance now." → **YES.**
140. **First rain after a long dry spell.** Proposal: "The road is most slippery now; I should slow down." → **YES.**
141. **A cyclist ahead wobbles near a drain grate / approaching a junction.** Proposal: "I should give extra space and delay overtaking." → **YES.**
142. **Children playing near a yard-street exit.** Proposal: "I should crawl at walking pace and be ready to stop." → **YES** (pihakatu rules + special caution).

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Match the exam blueprint.** Build your practice "traffic situations" mode to mirror Traficom's section exactly: 50 Yes/No image items per mock, ~10 seconds per item, fail if more than 7 wrong (43/50 pass). Keep the multiple-choice and risk-identification sections separate so users internalise the per-section pass logic (you cannot compensate a weak section with a strong one).

## Caveats
- **Official exam questions are secret.** Traficom does not publish its question bank; all practice items (yours included) must be original and rule-based, not copies. The scenarios here are constructed from the law and authoritative guidance, not from leaked items.
- **Transition-period markings.** Cycle-crossing (pyörätien jatke) markings and give-way signs were to be aligned by 1 June 2022, but not all locations complied; where a give-way sign is absent the cyclist yields. Build scenarios to teach "signs govern, not markings."
- **Source tiers.** Statutory points are anchored in Tieliikennelaki 729/2018 and Traficom/Väylä/Poliisi/Liikenneturva. A few interpretive nuances (e.g., tram-from-behind turning cases) rest on case law (KKO) and driving-school commentary; these are noted as judgement items rather than black-letter certainties.
- **The 10-second image timing** comes from official guidance documents and consistent user reports, not the current live Traficom web page — verify before stating it as a present-day published figure.
- **Numbers current as of June 2026.** Winter-tyre dates and tread depth, the 0.5‰ drink-drive limit, and default speed limits are stable and were re-confirmed against Traficom and Yle, but always reconcile against the latest Traficom/Finlex text before release.
